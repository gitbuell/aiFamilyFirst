# Comprehensive NP Intake & Assessment Data Requirements

## The Gap: What We Have vs. What You Actually Need

**Current System Captures:**
- Patient name, age, baseline medications, allergies
- New prescriptions, drug interactions
- Simple medication instructions for family

**Missing for Real Clinical Practice:**
- Vital signs (BP, HR, temp, respiratory rate, O2 sat)
- Chief complaint and history of present illness (HPI)
- Past medical history details (when diagnosed, current management, complications)
- Physical examination findings
- Labs and imaging results (with dates, reference ranges)
- Severity/acuity assessment
- Risk stratification
- Differential diagnosis
- Assessment and plan for THIS visit
- Red flags and contraindications specific to the condition

### SCENARIO 1: Patient with Hypertension (Common Chronic Disease)

#### Current Family_Profiles Only Captures:
- Baseline medication: Lisinopril 10 mg daily
- Baseline allergies: Penicillin

#### What an NP ACTUALLY Needs:

**Patient Demographics & Comorbidities:**
- Age, sex, race/ethnicity (risk factors differ by population)
- Smoking status (active, former, never)
- Alcohol use (drinks/week)
- Family history of HTN, MI, stroke, kidney disease
- Obesity status (BMI, waist circumference)
- Diabetes? Kidney disease? Other cardiovascular risk factors?

**BP Management History:**
- When diagnosed with HTN? (2010? 2020?)
- Previous BP readings (trend over time, not just today)
  - BP at last visit (1 week ago): 128/82
  - BP 1 month ago: 132/88
  - BP 6 months ago: 138/92
  - Are we trending better or worse?
- Target BP for this patient? (Maybe <130/80, or <140/90 if older/frail)
- How well controlled? (At goal? Off goal? Worsening?)

- Chest pain? Shortness of breath? Neurological symptoms? (Suggests hypertensive emergency)
- Recent medication non-adherence? (Often reason for sudden spike)
- Medication interactions? (NSAIDs + ACE inhibitor = dangerous)
- Salt intake? Obesity? Sleep apnea? (Modifiable factors)

### SCENARIO 2: New Diagnosis Workup—Possible Cancer

This is where the current system COMPLETELY fails.

#### What You'd Need to Capture:

**Chief Complaint & History of Present Illness:**
- Why did patient present? ("Lump in breast," "persistent cough," "blood in stool")
- How long? When first noticed? Growing? Painful?
- Associated symptoms?
  - Fatigue? Weight loss? Fevers? Night sweats?
  - Cough with hemoptysis?
  - GI symptoms (constipation, diarrhea, obstruction)?
- Risk factors for THIS cancer?
  - Breast cancer: Age, family history, menopause status, HRT use, prior biopsies
  - Lung cancer: Smoking history (pack-years), asbestos exposure, family history
  - Colon cancer: Age, family history, polyp history, IBD, diet

**Red Flags that Change Everything:**
- Unintentional weight loss >10 lbs in 3 months
- Night sweats soaking through clothes
- Persistent fever without infection
- Progressive weakness/fatigue
- Hemoptysis, hematochezia (blood in stool), hematuria (blood in urine)

**Imaging/Biopsy Results (If Available):**
- CT chest: "2.3 cm nodule in right lower lobe, suspicious for malignancy"
- Biopsy: "Pending" or "Adenocarcinoma of lung"
- Staging: If cancer confirmed, what stage? (Stage 1 vs. Stage 4 changes everything)

**Labs:**
- CBC (white count, hemoglobin, platelets—cancer often affects these)
- CMP (liver function, kidney function—baseline for chemo)
- LDH (elevated in certain cancers)
- Tumor markers (PSA for prostate, CA-125 for ovarian, CEA for colon, etc.)

**Referral & Follow-up:**
- Needs oncology consult? Urgent vs. routine?
- Does NP need to expedite workup or reassure patient while awaiting specialist?
- What safety-net instructions? (Call back if: worsening symptoms, fever, bleeding)

**Patient Education Needs:**
- Is this cancer or is this "probably benign"? (Changes messaging to family)
- Does child need to know? Does spouse know? (Very different from "take your allergy pill")

### SCENARIO 3: Acute Injury—Broken Toe

Much simpler, but still needs specific data.

#### What You'd Need:

**Mechanism of Injury:**
- How did it happen? When?
- Direct trauma? (Dropped weight, stubbed) Or stress fracture? (Overuse)
- Any swelling at time of injury? Any popping sound?

**Physical Exam Findings:**
- Which toe? (5th metatarsal fractures are different from phalanges)
- Swelling? Bruising? Deformity?
- Can patient bear weight? Walk?
- Neurovascular exam: Capillary refill? Sensation? Pulses intact?
- Is there malalignment? (If yes, might need reduction)

**Imaging:**
- X-rays: Simple fracture? Displaced? Intra-articular?
- OR no fracture, just soft tissue injury?

**Treatment Decision:**
- Stress fracture vs. acute break (treatment totally different)
- Needs closed reduction under anesthesia? Or just tape/buddy splint?
- Weight-bearing allowed? Or strict non-weight-bearing for 6 weeks?

**Red Flags:**
- Severe neurovascular compromise (needs urgent intervention)
- Open fracture (needs antibiotics, possible OR)
- Compartment syndrome developing (rare but deadly)

**Follow-up:**
- Return precautions: When to call back?
- Physical therapy after healing?
- Activity restrictions?
